# 💡 Moriba call — questions to ask, and the hard ones coming back

The guide's rules stand: listening call, 3 questions max on the day, no pitching.
This is the menu to pick from, plus the incoming fire to be ready for.

## 5 questions the founder should ask (pick 3 on the day)

1. **"Where does the current approach to tracking and behavior actually break — what
   does everyone get wrong?"** *(keep — from the guide; opens the rant, and the rant is
   the jackpot)*
2. **"What would you need to see from someone before you believed their
   maneuver-detection work was real?"** *(keep — his answer is our credibility roadmap,
   and it tees up our checked-against-history number: suspects move 11.3× more than
   controls, 72% vs 11% over the bar — say it only if he asks what we've done)*
3. **"ASTRIAGraph set out to make orbit knowledge a public good. What stopped it from
   going further — data, money, or people not caring?"** *(proves we did the homework
   AND maps the failure modes of exactly the thing we're attempting)*
4. **"Public GP data is smoothed around maneuvers by design. Is behavior monitoring
   from public elements a dead end, or good enough to matter?"** *(our biggest
   technical risk, put to the person most likely to say the harsh version — better to
   hear it now than from a customer)*
5. **"Who else should I be learning from?"** *(keep — one name = three doors)*

Recommended trio if he's warm: **1, 2, 5**. If he's testing us: **3, 4, 5** — the
homework questions earn the right to the intro question.

## 3 hard questions he'll likely ask back — with honest answers

1. **"How is this making things better, and not just another company selling safety?"**
   → Honest answer: the monitoring runs on free public data and the founder's position,
   already sent to him in writing, is that *safety information in orbit shouldn't be a
   luxury good*. Concrete offer: the population-level "went quiet" list could be
   published openly, with paid tiers only for book-specific service. Don't promise
   open-sourcing everything — say what we'd actually do.
2. **"What have you actually verified? Anyone can rank a list."**
   → Honest answer: one real out-of-sample check — flagged suspects moved 11.3× more
   than controls against history we didn't fit on — and **zero** candidates checked
   against operator maneuver records yet. Say the word *unvalidated* before he does.
   Verification is our named bottleneck and Tim's whole job.
3. **"You're one person with no aerospace background. Why you?"**
   → Honest answer: no credential claim. The claim is speed and honesty of the
   pipeline: first light to a validated-against-history detector in under a week, every
   number reproducible from a script, and a tool that returns zero on a quiet day
   rather than inventing findings. Then stop talking — don't over-defend.

## Recommendation

Do the promised homework first (TED talk, ASTRIAGraph, Wayfinder — it's already on
[[01 TASKS]]); question 3 above only works if it's real. Write the chosen 3 questions
on paper before the call, and rehearse saying "unvalidated" out loud once.
