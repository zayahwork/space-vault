# 💡 Who else pays for "this satellite went quiet" — besides insurers

Same signal, five other wallets. Ranked by **how fast they could say yes**, not by
ceiling — sequence B already says insurers are the ceiling and the slow yes.

## 1. 🛰️ Constellation operators watching *other people's* satellites — fastest yes
**Pain:** a dead satellite stops dodging. Conjunction screening assumes the other guy
maneuvers; a neighbor that quietly died is a hazard nobody flagged. Operators can't see
competitors' health — we can, from public data, no permission needed.
**Speed:** fast — they already buy SSA tools, budget exists, a $10–25K add-on is a
credit-card decision. Also our declared validation customers, so the ask is natural.

## 2. 📡 Ground-station / comms providers (KSAT, SSC, AWS Ground Station resellers)
**Pain:** they schedule contacts and capacity against satellites that may be dead;
revenue forecasting on a customer whose spacecraft went quiet is fiction. Early warning
= renegotiate or resell capacity sooner.
**Speed:** fast-ish — commercial, unregulated, small dollars, but we'd be teaching them
a pain they haven't named yet. One discovery call would settle it.

## 3. 🏦 Space-sector investors / equity analysts (and debt lenders on constellations)
**Pain:** an operator's press releases say "nominal" long after the fleet stops
station-keeping. Independent behavioral truth about a portfolio company's assets is
diligence gold — the short-seller/lender version of the insurer pitch.
**Speed:** medium — they buy data readily and cheaply (one report, $5–15K), but
episodic, not recurring. Good for cash and credibility quotes, not a base.

## 4. 🪖 Government SDA (USSF/SDA, allied agencies) via SBIR or prime subcontract
**Pain:** "pattern-of-life change" is literally the mission — an adversary satellite
going quiet (or a friendly one dying) is exactly what they watch for. Our
[[Guide - SBIR Steps]] watch task already covers the entry door.
**Speed:** slow — no open topic today (`sbir_scan.py` says zero), 6–18 month cycles.
Highest strategic value, worst time-to-yes. Stay parked until the watcher fires.

## 5. ⚖️ Regulators / debris-compliance (FCC post-mission disposal, ESA Zero Debris)
**Pain:** FCC now requires 5-year disposal; a satellite that stopped maneuvering is a
future compliance failure. Someone must notice — today, effectively nobody does at
population scale.
**Speed:** slowest — government procurement, mandate ambiguity. Watch, don't chase.

## Recommendation

Nothing here changes sequence B. But **#1 is the same conversation we're already having
with operators** — add one line to operator outreach: *"we'll also tell you which
satellites near yours have gone quiet."* Free to test, and it turns the validation
customers into the first paying wedge for the quiet signal. #3 is worth one
experimental email once the Jul 29 capability is real.
