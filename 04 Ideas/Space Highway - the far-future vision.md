# 🛣️ The Space Highway idea

> Captured 2026-07-20, founder's own idea during Day 1. **Parked on purpose** — this is the
> far-future layer, way after Product 1 (maneuver detection) and even after the asteroid
> minerals dream. Written down so we never lose it. Plain English; terms in [[Glossary]].

## The idea (founder's words, cleaned up)

Build a "highway system" for space — organized lanes/rules so nothing collides — and give it
to everyone, free, as shared infrastructure.

## Reality check (why it's real AND why it's not now)

**It's a real concept.** The field calls it **STM (Space Traffic Management)**, and researchers
at MIT/Purdue have published actual "orbital lane/slotting" designs. The US government's free
TraCSS system is a baby first step. Moriba Jah — who we emailed today — is the world's loudest
advocate for exactly this vision. Good company to be in.

**The three walls that keep it from existing yet:**
1. **Physics** — orbital paths *must* cross each other (a pole-to-pole orbit and an equatorial
   orbit intersect twice every lap, forever). You can assign altitude "shells," but you can't
   paint lanes that never touch. A highway reduces chaos; it can't eliminate crossings.
2. **Junk** — over half of everything up there is dead **debris** that can't steer. Rules only
   govern the living. The drunk drivers with no brakes stay on the road no matter what.
3. **Governance (the killer)** — space belongs to no one (**Outer Space Treaty**, 1967) and
   there's no cop. Making the US, China, Russia + every startup follow one lane system is a
   decades-long diplomacy project, not a software project. Proof it *can* happen slowly: **GEO**
   is already semi-highway-ified — the **ITU** assigns parking slots there — but that took ~50
   years and only works because GEO is one thin ring.

## Why this makes our CURRENT plan stronger (not a distraction)

Any highway needs one thing underneath before lanes mean anything:
**knowing what every object is doing, and instantly noticing when one breaks the pattern.**
Traffic cameras before traffic lanes.

That's literally the maneuver-detection / pattern-of-life engine we're building this week.
So the ladder now reads:

1. **Now:** the camera — detect + flag behavior from free public data (revenue: operators, SBIR).
2. **Later:** point the same detection brain at asteroids (minerals prospecting).
3. **Way later, when the time is ready:** the highway — our behavior layer becomes the
   monitoring/enforcement backbone of real space traffic rules. Give the basic layer to
   everyone; sell the advanced layer.

## The line to use in conversations (it lands well)

*"Long-term I think orbit ends up with traffic rules. I'm building the part that watches
whether traffic is behaving."*
