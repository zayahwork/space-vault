# 🎯 Guide — The Wedge Memo (1 page, no more)

**What it is:** one page that says exactly what we sell, to whom, and why us. It becomes the
seed of the SBIR proposal AND the sharper outreach pitch. Write it with Claude — but the
decisions in it are yours.

## The 4 sections

1. **Problem** — Free collision warnings exist (TraCSS), but nobody tells the ignored buyers
   *which warnings matter* or *whether that other object is behaving normally*. The premium
   version (Slingshot's Agatha) is enterprise-priced and sensor-bundled.
2. **Product** — ML that learns every object's routine from **free public catalog data** and
   flags the abnormal. OMM-native. No sensors, no hardware.
3. **Who pays** —
   - Space Force: their own 2026 language is "predictive SDA" (= this, verbatim) — via SBIR
   - Small operators: ~$25K/yr price point proven by Kayhan's public pricing
   - Insurers: unserved entirely (see [[Guide - Insurer Probe]] results)
4. **Why us** — ML is the hard part and it's our strength; $0 data cost; MIT SPLID benchmark
   to prove capability fast; solo = cheap = can serve the customers incumbents can't afford to.

## Rules
- **One page.** If it doesn't fit, we don't understand it yet.
- Every claim must be true and checkable — this doc feeds government proposals.
- Update it after every real conversation; it's a living page, not a monument.
