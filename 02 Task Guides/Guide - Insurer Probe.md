# 🕵️ Guide — The Insurance Probe (the sleeper wedge)

**The idea:** space insurers price satellite missions (premiums run 5–10% of a mission's budget
in crowded orbits!) but nobody sells them data on how the *neighborhood* around an insured
satellite actually behaves. One conversation tells us if this market is real. If it is, we may
have a wedge with literally zero competitors.

## Find ONE contact (no LinkedIn — use company pages)
Check these firms' "space" practice pages — they list team names and often emails:
- **AXA XL** — space insurance practice
- **Marsh** — space projects team
- **Gallagher / Lockton** — aerospace practices
Or the firm's general contact form addressed to "the space team."

>

## What a good answer sounds like
"We basically can't get that data" = 💰. "We don't care, actuarial tables cover it" = wedge
dead for insurers, which is also worth knowing. Log the answer in [[Guide - Handling Replies]].
