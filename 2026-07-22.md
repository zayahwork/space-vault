Three jobs, all landed:

   **OneWeb: clean win** (80% of suspects verified vs 10% baseline) — so the method isn't
   a Starlink fluke. **GEO** (Intelsat, SES — the big insured satellites ~36,000 km up):
   the pipeline runs, Intelsat looks promising, **SES shows no signal yet** — partly
   because GEO burns barely change altitude, which is the thing our verifier watches. We
   said the miss out loud in writing before anyone else could find it.
   → [[RESULTS - Beyond Starlink]]

3. **The daily run is automated.** A new scheduled task (**Maneuver Alert**, every 6h,
   30 min after each archive snapshot) scores all four constellations against their fixed
   alert bars and appends the verdict to [[RESULTS - Alert Log]] — including "quiet,
   nothing happened" days. Nobody has to remember anything; **the July 29 demo week is
   now building itself.**

## Jargon, once
- **bar / baseline** = what a normal orbit-vs-catalog gap looks like, learned from past
  days and frozen; only something *beyond* that gets flagged.
- **verified** = a second, unrelated method (30 days of Space-Track altitude history) also
  saw movement near the same time.
