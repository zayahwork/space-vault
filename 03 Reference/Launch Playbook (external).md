# 🚀 Launch Playbook — the Okara method

> [!warning] This is somebody else's writing, not ours
> Captured from a public post by a founder at **Okara** (`@askOkara`), saved verbatim for
> reference. Their claims, their numbers, their track record — not ours. If we ever quote or
> adapt this publicly, credit them. The source text below **cuts off mid-sentence** at "it starts
> with the product?" — the full list of steps is there, but the detail under each step is missing.
> Worth tracking down the rest before we rely on it.

## Their claims (as stated)

- 100M+ views across launches, **no agency, no ad spend**
- Okara launch: launch tweet did **14M views in a day**, **3M visitors** during the launch
- 3 weeks before that: another launch, **70M views in 3 weeks**, all organic
- 6 weeks before that: another launch, **1M organic views**

## The actual argument (this is the useful part)

> "A viral launch looks like luck. It isn't." Every effortless-looking launch is weeks or months
> of preparation, and **the preparation is surprisingly mechanical.**

Their loop during a year of pivots: **build a product → launch → get feedback → decide the
product's fate.** The point of launching is not applause, it's compression:

> "A launch is everything on steroids — you get thousands of users and real feedback that without
> a launch would usually take months or years."

Their method for building the checklist: write the simple list of what has to happen before a
launch, then add sub-tasks, then sub-sub-tasks, **until every item is atomic.** Then just work it.
(That is exactly how [[01 TASKS]] is already structured — same instinct, different domain.)

## The launch requisites (their list, verbatim)

1. product
2. press release
3. launch video
4. day and time
5. influencers, users, friends
6. copywriting skills
7. X thread
8. launch day
9. newsletter
10. afterward

## 🧭 Where we actually are (our annotation, be honest)

**We cannot run this playbook.** Step 1 is "product," and we have a v0.2 detector with two named
blind spots, no benchmark score, and zero users. Running a launch sequence now would be a loud
announcement of something that doesn't work yet — the same mistake as the [[Kelso Reading - Digest|3D
globe]]: polish attached to an unproven core invites exactly the scrutiny we can't survive.

**But one idea here transfers immediately, and it's the reason this note exists.**

### 💡 Launch the *finding*, not the product

We already have something launch-shaped that requires no product at all:

> **The median time from launch to a trackable orbit has more than quadrupled since 2019 —
> 0.65 days to 2.71 days — and it has risen every year since 2021. Provable from public data,
> on a laptop, for free.** (`06 Code/launch_id_delay.py`)

That is data journalism, not a product pitch. It costs nothing, it's true, it's reproducible, and
it reaches exactly the audience we need. It builds the credential *while* the detector is still
being fixed. Most of the requisites above map onto it cleanly — thread, chart, day/time, afterward
— and the ones that don't (press release, launch video) simply get skipped.

The finding is built entirely on **Kelso's** published data, and he is a live contact who replied
