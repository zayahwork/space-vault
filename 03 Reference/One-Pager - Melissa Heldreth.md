# 🎯 One-Pager — Melissa Heldreth (Gallagher US Space Practice, Seattle)

## Who she is

**Area SVP & Director, U.S. Space Practice, Arthur J. Gallagher & Co.** Appointed **December
2025** — the practice is brand new and she's building it. Based in **Seattle**.

Career arc is pure space/aero broking: Commercial Account Manager at Lockton Affinity → Head
of Space & Technology Division Advisor at **AHT Insurance** → practice leader, aerospace &
technology, **The Baldwin Group** → Gallagher. That's ~four rungs of increasingly senior
space-risk broking, not a generalist parachuted in.

## Why she matters to us — three reasons

1. **A broker sees every deal.** Underwriters each see their own book; the broker
   intermediates between operators and *all* the capacity. One conversation with her maps
   which underwriters care about on-orbit behavior data, who's pricing maneuver-frequency
   clauses, and which operators are complaining about what.
2. **New-in-role means she needs things.** A director building a practice from scratch needs
   market intelligence, local relationships, and things that make her clients smarter. A free,
   verified read on "which satellites near your insured are actually behaving abnormally" is
   the kind of thing a broker can carry into a client meeting.
3. **She's local.** Gallagher's announcement placed her in Seattle. The founder can meet her
   at a Seattle-area space event without it being an ask at all.

## What Gallagher's space practice covers

End-to-end satellite/space program coverage: launch and in-orbit hull, liability. Gallagher
Specialty also publishes **Plane Talking**, the market's most-read public aerospace report —
the practice has a publishing culture, which matters for us (see the play below).

## The warm path (in order of preference)

1. **In person, Seattle.** Space & industry events — anything Space Northwest / local
   new-space meetups / UW aero events. New practice directors go to these *to be found*.
   No email needed; "I build maneuver-detection tooling from free public data" is a
   conversation, not a pitch.
2. **Via the publication.** When the launch-delay finding publishes ([[Publish - Launch Delay
   Finding]]), it is precisely broker-newsletter material (quantified, public-data, risk-
   adjacent). A note *after* it's public — "thought your clients might find this useful" —
   gives value before asking anything.
3. **Via a shared contact.** Kunstadter (AXA XL) sits on the Office of Space Commerce ACES
   committee; TraCSS (row #40, day-1) is that office. If the TraCSS thread ever warms, the
   insurance world is two steps away. Slow, but real.

**Not the path:** a cold email now. One shot with the person who can introduce us to the
whole market — spend it when there's something finished to show (the wedge memo at minimum,
ideally the published finding).
