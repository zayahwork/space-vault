I read your cislunar space-traffic surveillance paper with Frueh and Howell. My problem is much less exotic - LEO, public data, one person - but the estimation difficulty underneath it seems to be the same one you spend your time on.

Two of my own methods agreeing, not operator ground truth - I'd rather say that than have you find it.

Here is the part I can't resolve on my own. I have no covariance and no maneuver log, so I substituted a matched control group and treat its ~10% hit rate as an empirical false-positive rate. From an estimation-theory standpoint, is that a legitimate stand-in for a calibrated statistic, or a number that will quietly mislead me as the population changes?
