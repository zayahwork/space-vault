## operator — small teams drowning in alerts that aren't real

**Their pain, their words:** "another conjunction warning, and it's somebody's
evening again." On a small constellation team, screening alerts land on named
people, not a department — and most apparent maneuvers in the public catalog
are stale elements, not spacecraft. Alert fatigue is a staffing problem
wearing a data-quality costume.
**The number for them:** the funnel first — about five in six apparent
maneuvers are just old data — then the primary sentence, dated July 22
(~68–72% of top suspects vs ~10% of matched controls). The stale-data ratio is
the part they live with daily; the verified rate is why they should believe us.
**Caveat, their level:** plain-spoken, one clause: "two of my own methods
agreeing, not operator ground truth." Operators know exactly what ground truth
is — they own it. Never imply we have theirs.
**Ask:** 15 minutes on how they find out today when a neighbour starts
behaving differently — judgement or tooling. Discovery, not a sale; nothing to
buy exists.

## insurer — pricing on-orbit risk with launch-era information

**Their pain, their words:** premiums are priced on launch and hull heritage;
once the asset is on station, the behaviour of the neighbourhood around it is
nobody's data feed. A broker's edge is exactly the fact the underwriter didn't
have.
**The number for them:** the primary sentence, dated July 22, with the control
group spelled out in civilian words: "compared against ordinary satellites
checked the same way — ~68–72% versus ~10%." Numerate audience, not an
orbital-mechanics one; the existence of a control group IS the credibility.
If the conversation reaches the big insured birds: GEO corroboration is under active validation against documented maneuvers.
**Caveat, their level:** insurers price uncertainty for a living — give it to
them straight and briefly: "measured on public data, two of my own methods
agreeing, not operator ground truth; I'd rather say that up front than
oversell it."
**Ask:** one market question we cannot answer from outside: when underwriters
price in-orbit risk, what do they actually get told about the behaviour of
objects around the insured — is anyone pricing on it? 15 minutes or coffee.
(Segment is founder-gated: every row is hold — drafting only.)

## partner — SSA vendors who already know where the bodies are buried

**Their pain, their words:** public GP data is smoothed around maneuvers by
design, and the labelled datasets everyone trains on are partly simulated.
They have sensors and pipelines; their open question is coverage and labels,
same as everyone's.
**The number for them:** full methodological form, dated July 22: ~68–72% of
top suspects over a movement bar that only ~10% of matched controls clear —
controls matched on shell, altitude band and epoch window, bar set at the
controls' own 90th percentile. This audience will probe the construction;
giving it before they ask is the pitch.
**Caveat, their level:** stated as a method limitation and an invitation:
"two of my own methods agreeing, not operator ground truth — and I'd genuinely
like to know where a public-catalog-only approach breaks in ways your sensors
can see." Never pose as a buyer; the posture is comparing notes.
**Ask:** does our approach overlap what they already do, or cover a blind spot
in it? 15 minutes, we bring the worked example and the failure modes.

## academic — the no-labels problem is their open problem too

**Their pain, their words:** there is no public ground truth for maneuvers;
labelled sets are simulated or tiny; TLE epochs hide the very information a
detector needs. Every maneuver-detection paper ends with some version of "we
lack validation data."
**The number for them:** the primary sentence, dated July 22 (~68–72% of top
suspects vs ~10% of matched controls), framed as an empirical base-rate
methodology they can critique: matched control group, bar at the controls'
90th percentile, the ~10% as an empirical false-positive proxy. Offer the data
and the failure modes — it's all free public data, nothing to be cagey about.
**Caveat, their level:** the precise form: "two of my own methods agreeing on
differently-shaped data, not operator ground truth — I'd rather say that than
have a reviewer find it."
**Ask:** ONE specific question about THEIR paper — the template's placeholder
line exists precisely because a generic email to a researcher is a wasted
address. If the draft doesn't cite their result, it isn't ready.
