Subject: a question small operators rarely get asked — with a number attached

Hi — written for John Curry or the mission operations team; thanks to whoever passes it along.

I built a maneuver detector that runs entirely on free public tracking data, and checked it with a second method: 30 days of altitude history against a matched control group. As of July 22, ~68–72% of the top suspects show a real, independently-checked burn signature versus ~10% of matched controls. Caveat up front — two of my own methods agreeing, not operator ground truth.

Most tooling in this field is priced for people flying hundreds of satellites. I'm interested in the other end, which is why I'm writing to Sidus rather than the obvious names: operators with a handful of birds get the same warnings without a team to absorb them. And per my measurements, about five in six of those apparent maneuvers are stale catalog data, not movement.

When you get a warning, or something near one of your satellites behaves oddly — what actually happens? Who looks, and with what?

Fifteen minutes would help me a lot, and I have nothing to sell.
