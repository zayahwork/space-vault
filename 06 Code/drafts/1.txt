Hi,

I built a maneuver detector that runs on free public tracking data, and then checked it with a second, independent method — 30 days of altitude history, with a matched control group. The suspects it flags move 11.3× more than satellites it calls ordinary, and 72% of them show a real burn signature against 11% of controls. The honest caveat: that's two of my own methods agreeing, not operator ground truth.

The finding underneath is the one I suspect matters to a fleet Planet's size: about five of six apparent maneuvers in the public catalog aren't maneuvers at all, just stale element sets. If your conjunction screening consumes that data, a lot of the noise is avoidable by construction.

I'd like to test that against reality: when something near one of your satellites changes its behaviour, how do you find out today — a tool, or a person watching screens?
